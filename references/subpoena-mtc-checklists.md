# Subpoena MTC Checklists

Use these issue checklists when drafting California subpoena MTC materials.

## Subpoena Basics

Check:

- subpoenaed deponent
- records subject/account holder
- production date
- service date
- proof of service
- request text
- accounts/date ranges/transactions
- whether consumer notice is actually required
- whether the deponent or an account holder/objector raised the issue
- whether the custodian produced, refused, held production because of objections, or remained silent

## Objection Posture

Identify:

- objector identity and standing theory
- whether the objector is the custodian, account holder, party, nonparty, consumer, or employee
- whether the objections are global or request-specific
- whether the custodian has its own objections or only a production hold
- whether there is a protective order or confidentiality protocol already in place
- sanctions target and requested amount

## Common Objections

Track and respond to:

- relevance
- overbreadth
- burden/oppression
- proportionality
- reasonable particularity
- attorney-client privilege
- attorney work product
- privacy
- confidential/proprietary information
- trade secret
- opposing counsel/harassment
- notice to consumer
- service timing
- second bite / reissued subpoena / timeliness

## Privilege And Work Product

For bank records:

- distinguish objective third-party bank records from legal advice, privileged communications, invoices, litigation files, attorney mental impressions, and legal research
- use only supplied or authority-table authorities
- use prior court rulings or transcripts if supplied
- address misuse of authorities if the same arguments were previously rejected

## Privacy

For business entities:

- identify whether the claimant is a natural person or business entity
- analyze the need for discovery and tracing
- use balancing only with supplied authorities
- identify existing protective-order terms and redaction requirements
- avoid offering redactions as the opening position unless the user wants that; frame tailoring as something the Court may order if it believes additional protections are necessary

## Procedural Objections

For consumer notice:

- determine whether the records subject is a statutory consumer
- distinguish businesses from consumers where supported

For service timing:

- identify the rule that applies to business-records subpoenas
- compare issuance, service, and production dates
- distinguish objections raised by the deponent from objections raised by a non-deponent account holder

For second-bite arguments:

- compare whether there was a prior subpoena to the same deponent for the same records
- compare whether a prior motion deadline expired
- distinguish a single timely subpoena from a reissued subpoena designed to reset the clock

## Separate Statement

Before drafting:

- decide whether subpoena categories are one request or multiple requests
- verify exact request text by OCR/visual review
- use original objection text, not summaries
- include page:line cites for objections when available
- keep good-cause response concise and consolidated
- mirror the MPA structure without repeating the full MPA argument

## Sanctions

Track:

- statutory basis supplied by user or authority table
- who opposed or maintained objections
- whether the custodian was silent or refused
- substantial justification
- meet-and-confer history
- requested amount
- declaration support for attorney time and rate
- whether sanctions should target the custodian, objector, counsel, or no one
